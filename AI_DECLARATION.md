# AI Assistance & Transparency Declaration

This repository contains the official implementation of our paper on Graph Out-of-Distribution (OOD) Detection. In the spirit of rigorous academic transparency and modern software engineering practices, the authors would like to declare the use of Large Language Models (LLMs) and AI coding assistants (e.g., Cursor, Claude 3.5, ChatGPT) during the development of this codebase.

## 1. Core Academic Independence

The theoretical framework, algorithmic pipeline, mathematical formulations, and experimental protocols were **entirely conceived, designed, and directed by the human authors**. The core scientific innovations—including the Asymmetric Inference architecture, the Cross-Modal Supervised Contrastive Distillation mechanism, and the Free-Energy calibration operators—are the original intellectual property of the researchers.

## 2. The Role of AI Assistants

AI tools were utilized strictly as "pair-programming assistants" to accelerate the engineering pipeline. Their limited scope of involvement includes:
* Translating pre-defined mathematical logic into PyTorch tensor operations.
* Generating boilerplate code for data loading and metric logging (e.g., CSV/JSON exports).
* Enforcing strict PEP 8 coding standards and adding Python type hints.
* Drafting and standardizing Google-style docstrings for better codebase readability.

## 3. Human Oversight & Responsibility

**AI did not invent the methodologies or fabricate any experimental data.** Every line of code generated or refactored by AI has undergone rigorous human review, line-by-line logical verification, and extensive empirical testing across multiple random seeds. 

The authors take **full and absolute responsibility** for the accuracy, security, reproducibility, and scientific validity of this repository and the reported experimental results.
